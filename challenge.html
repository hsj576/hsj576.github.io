Background

By 2023, the global market size for edge intelligence is projected to reach $3.093 billion, with a CAGR of 28.8%. However, the real value lies in the diverse array of industries that edge intelligence can revolutionize, including transportation, industry, energy, aerospace, media, agriculture, and beyond. Despite this potential, there are still challenges hindering the scalability of edge intelligence, such as the difficulties posed by small sample sizes and heterogeneous data at the edge. Additionally, customers of edge intelligence demand both data security compliance and offline autonomy.

Technical Challenges

  • Catastrophic forgetting: The use of a single-task approach to build a model for edge inference in the cloud, such as isolated learning, single-task incremental learning, and single-task federated learning, can lead to catastrophic forgetting when updating the model, resulting in the difficulty of achieving personalized services for each user.
  • AI engineering and automation: The use of a multi-task approach, such as multi-task learning, multi-task incremental learning, and multi-task federated learning, by defining multiple models manually to adapt to different edge nodes, requires significant investment in resources and manpower for the construction and maintenance of multiple customized models, indicating a long way to go for AI engineering and automation.
  • Unknown task recognition and processing: The complex and diverse edge environment often does not conform to the closed hypothesis that all tasks learned perfectly during the training phase will be present during the inference phase. It is essential to assume the existence of unknown tasks during the operation of edge models, and to update the models promptly to ensure the robustness and reliability of related services.

    • Research Topics and Benchmarks

    We conducted research on the core technologies and system optimization of edge-cloud collaborative lifelong learning based on the Sedna and Ianvs frameworks, achieving multi-model full lifecycle management. System Goals and Core Technologies:

  • Maximize the unknown task generalization indicator (FWT) while maintaining a forgetting resistance indicator (BWT) of no less than 95%.
  • Shorten the development time for new scenarios to within two weeks, ensuring that data does not leave the edge and that services operate autonomously offline.
  • Decoupling of models, enabling plug-and-play models in complex and changing edge scenarios.
  • Automatic task definition and allocation: Divide latent tasks in a given dataset and match inference samples to the most similar task. The task matching accuracy indicator reaches 95%.
  • Unknown task recognition: Distinguish unknown and known tasks during inference, with an accuracy indicator of 99%.
  • Unknown task processing: Utilize multi-source data and model optimization knowledge bases, such as mechanistic models, task migration discovery, simulation data, and large models in the cloud, to achieve forgetting resistance (BWT), generalization (FWT), and other indicators of 95%.
